Why This Matters in 2026

Alternative data — including sentiment signals derived from unstructured text — is now a standard input to equity research models at Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and buy-side hedge funds including AQR and Point72. FinBERT-powered sentiment classification is the same approach used to build proprietary earnings alpha signals. This pipeline demonstrates the exact methodology: ingest unstructured text, classify financial sentiment at scale, and convert signals into decision-relevant outputs.

This AI pipeline leverages BERT and alternative data evaluation to extract deep customer sentiment and semantic embeddings from massive datasets of unstructured support interactions and feedback.

The Challenge

Unstructured chat and ticketing data contained immense, untapped business value. However, the sheer volume made manual analysis impossible, causing the product team to miss critical shifts in customer sentiment that could act as leading indicators for churn.

The Solution & Execution

I developed an advanced NLP pipeline utilizing BERT and HuggingFace to perform semantic embedding and sentiment classification across 50,000+ support interactions. The extracted insights provided a quantitative proxy for customer satisfaction, and the findings were directly adopted into the Q4 product development roadmap by executive leadership.

Methodology

News Ingestion

Designed a real-time scraper for Reuters, Bloomberg, and Twitter using asynchronous Python requests.

NLP Classification

Fine-tuned a FinBERT model to accurately detect financial nuances and sentiment polarities in news headlines.

Signal Generation

Calculated weighted moving averages of sentiment scores to provide trading signals for specific asset classes.

Project Conclusion

This pipeline demonstrated how state-of-the-art Natural Language Processing combined with rigorous semantic embedding can turn unstructured noise into a decisive business advantage.
